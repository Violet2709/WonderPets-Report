Data Gathering

Instruments & Documentation

All consent forms, respondent profiles, survey instruments, and evidence gathered during testing.

Informed Consent for Usability Testing

You are being invited to participate in a usability testing study conducted by students of Partido State University as part of their HCI2 course requirement. Your participation is voluntary and you may withdraw at any time without penalty.

Purpose of the Study

This study aims to evaluate the usability of our prototype by observing how users interact with the system and gathering feedback on its ease of use, efficiency, and overall experience.

What You Will Do

  • Complete a set of tasks using the provided prototype
  • Answer a post-test questionnaire (5–10 minutes)
  • Allow your interactions to be observed and noted by evaluators

Confidentiality

All collected data will be anonymized and used solely for academic purposes. Your personal information will not be shared publicly.

By participating in this study, you acknowledge that you have read and understood the above information and agree to take part voluntarily.

Medium [Issue Title — e.g., No confirmation feedback after actions] H1 · Visibility ▼

Description: Describe the feedback issue here. Explain which user actions lacked visual/auditory confirmation and what user behavior resulted.

Heuristic Violated: Nielsen Heuristic #1 — Visibility of system status. The system should keep users informed about what is going on through appropriate feedback.

Recommendation: Add toast notifications, loading indicators, or success/error banners to give users immediate feedback after every action.
Medium [Issue Title — e.g., Inconsistent button styles across screens] H4 · Consistency ▼

Description: Describe the inconsistency issue here. Which screens had different button styles and how did that confuse participants?

Heuristic Violated: Nielsen Heuristic #4 — Consistency and standards. Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing.

Recommendation: Establish and enforce a design system or style guide for all interactive components across the prototype.
Low [Issue Title — e.g., Small tap targets on mobile view] H8 · Aesthetics ▼

Description: Describe the tap-target or aesthetic issue here. Minor but noted by multiple participants.

Heuristic Violated: Nielsen Heuristic #8 — Aesthetic and minimalist design. Irrelevant information and poor spatial layout compete with relevant information and reduce relative visibility.

Recommendation: Increase minimum touch target sizes to at least 44×44px and increase spacing between interactive elements.
